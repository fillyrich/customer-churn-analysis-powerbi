#  Customer Churn Analysis & Retention Strategy (Power BI)

##  Project Overview

Customer churn is a critical challenge for subscription-based businesses. This project analyses customer behaviour to identify key drivers of churn and provides actionable strategies to improve customer retention.

Using Power BI, this dashboard transforms raw customer data into a structured, insight-driven analysis that answers three core business questions:

- What is the current churn situation?  
- Why are customers leaving?  
- Which customers are most at risk?  

---

##  Objectives

- Analyse churn patterns and customer behaviour  
- Identify key factors influencing churn  
- Segment high-risk customers  
- Provide data-driven business recommendations  

---

##  Dataset

The dataset contains customer-level information including:

- Demographics: Senior Citizen, Dependents  
- Account Details: Contract Type, Tenure  
- Services: Internet Service, Tech Support  
- Billing: Monthly Charges, Payment Method  
- Target Variable: Churn (Yes/No)  

---

##  Dashboard Structure

The dashboard is organised into four key sections, following a clear business storytelling approach.

---

##  1. Executive Summary

### Key Metrics:
- Total Customers: 7,043  
- Churn Rate: 26.54%  
- Average Monthly Charges: £64.76  
- Average Tenure: 32.37 months  

### Key Insights:
- Customers on month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rates  
- Customers with short tenure (< 12 months) are most likely to churn  
- Higher monthly charges are associated with increased churn  
- Customers on long-term contracts show significantly higher retention  
- Electronic payment methods and lack of add-on services are linked to higher churn  

### Business Recommendations:
- Encourage long-term contracts through incentives  
- Target new customers early to reduce churn  
- Review pricing strategy for high-charge segments  

---

##  2. Customer Behaviour & Lifecycle Analysis

### Key Insights:
- Majority of churn occurs within the first 12 months  
- Customers with longer tenure are significantly more stable  
- Electronic payment methods are associated with higher churn  
- Early customer engagement is critical to retention  

---

##  3. Service & Support Drivers

### Key Insights:
- Fiber optic customers have higher churn rates  
- Customers without tech support churn significantly more  
- Lack of add-on services is strongly associated with higher churn  

### Business Recommendations:
- Provide proactive tech support for high-risk customers  
- Improve service engagement through add-ons and bundles  
- Target fiber optic customers with retention strategies  

---

##  4. High-Risk Customer Segmentation

### Risk Model Criteria:
Customers are classified as high-risk based on:

- Short tenure (< 12 months)  
- Month-to-month contract  
- High monthly charges (> £70)  

### High-Risk Customer Profile:
- Month-to-month contract  
- Tenure less than 12 months  
- Monthly charges greater than £70  

These customers represent the highest priority segment for retention strategies.

---

##  Key Insights Summary

- Churn is strongly driven by contract type and customer lifecycle stage  
- Pricing sensitivity plays a significant role in churn behaviour  
- Customer engagement (services & support) reduces churn risk  
- Targeting high-risk customers enables more effective retention strategies
